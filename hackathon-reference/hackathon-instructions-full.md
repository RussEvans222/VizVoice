These are optional prompts; you're not required to choose one. But if one resonates, your solution could directly serve a community that matters. Submissions that choose an Equality Group prompt are automatically considered for the Equality Group Champion Award ($250 per person/team member).

## 1. Getting Started

* **Q: How do I register for the Builder Track?** Go to the A4G registration page and select "Builder Track" when prompted to choose your track. One person per team registers and creates the team — teammates join via the invite link generated after registration.
* **Q: How many people can be on a team?** Teams are a minimum of 2, maximum of 3. Solo submissions are not allowed.
* **Q: What is the difference between the Builder Track and the other tracks?** The Builder Track is for hands-on builders who want to create a working Salesforce app or automation. Other tracks (Analyst, Admin, etc.) focus on different skill sets. You can only be on one track per event.
* **Q: Do I need to be an engineer to join the Builder Track?** No. Admins, consultants, architects, and non-engineers are all welcome. Clicks-only builds are explicitly allowed and judged on the same criteria.
* **Q: Can I join a team after registration opens?** Yes, up until the submission deadline. The team lead sends you an invite link from the Devpost team page.
* **Q: Can I switch tracks after I register?** Track switches are allowed before the build period starts. After the build period opens, track changes are not permitted. Contact the event team if you need to switch.
* **Q: Is there a kickoff event or orientation session?** Yes. Details are posted in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR>. Watch that channel for Zoom links and calendar invites.
* **Q: What is the build period? When does it start and end?** The exact dates are published on the event Devpost page and in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR>. Build your app only within the official build window — see the Rules section for details.
* **Q: Where do I go if I have a question not answered here?** Post in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR> and a support volunteer will help you.
* **Q: Is my submission public?** Once you begin adding details to your project, they will be visible to other Agentforce for Good Hackathon participants.

## 3. Building

* **Q: What should I build?** Anything that runs on or integrates with Salesforce and solves a real business problem. The judging criteria reward innovation, impact, and execution — build something you'd actually want to use at work.
* **Q: Does my build have to be original / new?** Yes. You cannot submit a project you built before the hackathon build period started. Reusing your own prior work or submitting a project that already exists in production is not allowed. See the Rules section.
* **Q: Can I do a clicks-only build (no code)?** Yes. Flows, Agentforce, OmniStudio, and other declarative tools are fully supported. Judges evaluate what the build does, not how it was built.
* **Q: Do I need to use GitHub?** No. GitHub is optional. If you use it, link your repo in your Devpost submission. Code review is not part of the judging criteria.
* **Q: Does my build need to include a Claude / AI agent?** The Builder Track has a requirement that AI/Claude agent usage be reviewed and approved before submission. The specific requirement is posted in the event brief and in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR>. Build your agent, then request a review in the support channel before you submit — do not wait until the last hour.
* **Q: Why is the Claude agent review required?** It ensures agents behave safely and responsibly, and that submissions meet Salesforce's responsible AI guidelines. It is also a judging criterion. Early review gives you time to make adjustments if needed.
* **Q: Can I use external APIs in my build?** Yes. External API calls from Apex, Flows, or MuleSoft are all fine. Make sure any credentials or API keys are not hardcoded in submitted screenshots or videos.
* **Q: Can I use Voice features?** Yes, if you've had Voice enabled in your org (see Orgs & Setup). Voice builds are encouraged — they tend to score well on innovation.
* **Q: Can I use MuleSoft integrations?** Yes. See the Orgs & Setup section for how to get MuleSoft access.
* **Q: Can I use data from outside Salesforce?** Yes. You can import sample/mock data or integrate with external systems. Do not use real customer or employee PII in your build or your demo video.
* **Q: My build is broken the night before the deadline. Help?** Post in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR> immediately — do not wait. Support volunteers monitor the channel through the final hours.
* **Q: How do I ensure my work is grounded in Salesforce's responsible AI and accessible principles?** Use the Accessibility Expert and RAI Self-Check Skill in Claude Code. You can find them in the AI Expert Suite, but MD files will also be provided once orgs are distributed.
* **Q: Can we use Third-party tools?** Third-party tools (e.g., Heroku, external APIs, SDKs) **are allowed**, as long as:
    * You're authorized to use them
    * They're publicly available for app development
    * Your submission still meets all requirements
    * One important note: since this is an internal hackathon, please keep your submission and any components out of public-facing environments. Don't make anything publicly accessible.

## 4. Submitting

* **Q: What do I need to submit?** Use this checklist:
    * Devpost submission created and published (not just saved as draft)
    * Project title and description filled out
    * Team members added to the Devpost project
    * Demo video uploaded (5 minutes max, see below)
    * Salesforce org ID included in the submission
    * Any relevant links (GitHub, slides, etc.) added
    * Claude/AI agent review completed (if applicable)
    * Submitted before the deadline (the form locks automatically)
    * [Devpost Help Article](https://help.devpost.com/article/126-know-your-submission-steps#:~:text=Make%20sure%20it's%20no%20larger,%2C%20word%2C%20or%20apk%20file.)
* **Q: What are the video requirements?** Maximum 5 minutes. Show the working app — judges want to see it in action, not just slides. Screen recording with narration is fine. Upload to YouTube (unlisted is OK) or Vimeo and paste the link into Devpost.
* **Q: Any tips for the Devpost submission?** Write your description for a judge who has never seen your project. Lead with the problem you are solving, then explain your solution. The description is read before the video, so make it count. Use bullet points for the tech stack.
* **Q: Does everyone on the team need to submit, or just the team lead?** The team lead submits the project on behalf of the team. All team members should be added to the Devpost project so they appear in the submission. Members do not submit separately.
* **Q: Can I edit my submission after submitting?** Yes, up until the deadline. After the deadline the form locks and no changes are accepted.
* **Q: What happens after I submit?** You will get an email confirmation from Devpost. The judging team reviews submissions and contacts finalists. See Judging & Prizes for the timeline.
* **Q: I submitted but I don't see my project in the gallery. Is it submitted?** Check that you clicked "Publish" and not just "Save Draft." Draft submissions are not visible in the gallery and may not be reviewed. If you are unsure, post in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR>.
* **Q: The submission form crashed / I hit an error on Devpost. What do I do?** Try a different browser first. If the problem persists, post in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR> immediately with a screenshot — the team can accept submissions via alternate means if there is a platform issue before the deadline.
* **Q: What is the submission deadline?** The deadline is posted on the event Devpost page and pinned in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR>. It does not move. Set a reminder.

## 5. Builder Judging & Prizes

* **Q: How does judging work?** Judging happens in two rounds:
    * **Round 1:** All submissions are reviewed by judges using the 6 criteria below. Top submissions advance to Round 2.
    * **Round 2 (Finals):** Finalists present live (or via async video) to a panel of senior judges. Winners are announced after final deliberation.
* **Q: What are the 6 judging criteria?**

|Criterion|What judges look for|
|  ---  |  ---  |
|**Innovation**|Is this a new idea or a fresh approach to an existing problem?|
|**Impact**|Does it solve a real, meaningful problem? How broad is the impact?|
|**Execution**|Does it actually work? Is it polished and complete?|
|**Use of Platform**|Does it make good use of Salesforce features and capabilities?|
|**Responsible AI**|Is AI used safely, ethically, and with appropriate guardrails?|
|**Demo Quality**|Is the video clear, compelling, and within time limits?|


* **Q: What are the prizes?** Prize amounts are published on the event Devpost page. Check there for the current prize structure — amounts may be updated before the event.
* **Q: Are there special awards beyond the main prizes?** Yes. Special awards (e.g., Best Use of AI, Best Admin Build, Most Impactful) are announced in the event brief. Check the Devpost page and <#C0B7B3ZKHTR> for the current list.
* **Q: When will finalists be notified?** Finalists are contacted by email and via Devpost message after Round 1 judging completes. The timeline is posted on the Devpost page. If you haven't heard back by the posted date, check your spam folder and post in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR>.
* **Q: Will judges have access to my org?** Finalist judges may be granted read-only access to your hackathon org to explore the build. Make sure your org is clean and does not contain any sensitive data.
* **Q: Can I see the judges' feedback?** Summary feedback is provided to finalists. General judging notes may be shared after the event depending on the format.
* **Q: How are ties broken?** Judges discuss and vote. In case of a tie, the Innovation and Impact criteria carry extra weight.
* **Q: I can not participate, but I would like to judge.** We are still recruiting round 1 judges. If you have interest, please [submit this workflow](https://slack.com/shortcuts/Ft0B9RLKMQ03/d75a0fd2464c50685acfe2e568d36ca8) to participate. Note that judges can not participate in the hackathon. You will need to be able to commit ~2 hours of async time (you can do more if you want!) during the judging window:
    * Jul 21, 2026 - 08:00am ET - Round 1 Judging Begins
    * Jul 24, 2026 - 08:00pm ET - Round 1 Judging Closes

## 6. Rules & Eligibility

* **Q: Can I use AI tools (Copilot, ChatGPT, etc.) to help me build?** Yes, AI coding assistants are allowed. Your build must still be substantially your own work — using AI to generate your entire app verbatim is not in the spirit of the event. Judges assess originality and understanding during finals.
* **Q: When must I start building?** You may only build during the official build period. Code, flows, or configurations created before the build period opens cannot be included in your submission. You can do design and planning ahead of time.
* **Q: Can I enter both the Builder Track and another track?** No. Each participant can only be in one track. Being on a Builder Track team counts as your track entry.
* **Q: What counts as an "original" build?** Your project must have been created during the hackathon build window. It cannot be a fork of an existing production app, a prior hackathon project, or something you built for a customer before the event started. New features added on top of existing work during the build window are evaluated case-by-case — when in doubt, post in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR>.
* **Q: What if I accidentally lose data in my org?** Hackathon orgs do not have full backup/restore. Be careful with destructive operations (deleting fields, removing data, uninstalling packages). If you lose something critical, post in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR> — admins may be able to help but full restoration is not guaranteed.
* **Q: Can my team change members after we register?** Members can be added before the submission deadline. Members cannot be removed once added (for fairness in prize distribution). Changes after the deadline are not accepted.
* **Q: What happens if I miss the deadline?** Submissions are not accepted after the deadline. The Devpost form closes automatically. No exceptions are made for technical issues on your end — plan to submit at least an hour early.
* **Q: What if there is a Devpost platform outage near the deadline?** If there is a verified platform-wide Devpost outage, the event team will announce an extension in <#C0B7B3ZKHTR> and via email. Do not assume an extension — monitor the channel.
* **Q: Can I participate solo?** No. Builder Track requires teams of 2–3.
* **Q: Can I be on two teams?** No.
* **Q: Can I participate in both tracks?** No. Choose one.
* **Q: Can contractors participate?** No. Current full-time Salesforce employees only.
* **Q: When do I get my org?** Provisioned orgs are delivered on a rolling basis starting June 15. Instructions will be sent to your registered email.
* **Q: When does the submission form open?** June 20, 2026 at 9:00 AM ET. You can start building June 8, but the submission form opens June 20. Save drafts as you go.
* **Q: What if my video is longer than 5 minutes?** Judges are not required to watch beyond 5 minutes. Keep it tight.
* **Q: What happens to my project after the hackathon?** Top projects may be considered for Salesforce Labs or internal product adoption. All IP remains yours, subject to Salesforce's standard employee IP policies.

## 📄 Type A: AI Concept Brief — Specific FAQs

**Do I need to build a prototype for Type A?** No. Type A is design-only. You're proposing an AI solution, not building it. However, your brief must be detailed enough that someone else could build the solution based on your description.

**How detailed does my solution design need to be?** You should explain:

* What inputs the AI solution takes
* What outputs it produces
* What the human's role is in the workflow
* What AI capabilities power the solution (generation, classification, summarization, reasoning, etc.)
* What would happen if this were implemented at scale

**Can I propose a solution that already exists?** You can propose an improvement or variation of an existing solution, but it must have **original elements**. "An AI hiring agent that does exactly what exists today" is not original.

**What if my concept is too big to implement in real life?** That's okay — this is a concept brief, not a business plan. You can propose something ambitious. However, judges will evaluate whether your impact hypothesis is **credible**, so be ambitious but grounded.

**Can I include visuals or mockups in my Type A brief?** Yes, and it's recommended. A simple diagram, flowchart, or mockup helps judges visualize your concept. A hand-drawn sketch or basic slide is fine as long as it's clear.

## 🤝 Type B: Human-AI Collaboration Showcase — Specific FAQs

**What counts as a "real workflow" for Type B?** A real workflow means:

* You actually ran it during the build period (June 8–July 20)
* You used an approved AI tool to accomplish something related to accessibility, equity, or inclusion
* You can show prompts you wrote, outputs the AI generated, and how you evaluated/refined those outputs
* It's not hypothetical — you did this work

**Can I document a workflow I use regularly at work?** Only if you run it fresh during the build period. You cannot retroactively document something you did before June 8. However, if you regularly use an AI workflow in your job, you can **run it again** during the build period on a new task and document that iteration.

**What if the workflow I ran didn't work perfectly?** That's actually good. Judges want to see your iteration and adaptation — what went wrong, how you adjusted, what you learned. A showcase where everything worked perfectly on the first try may actually be less compelling.

**How many rounds of iteration do I need to show?** Minimum: **two full rounds** of human-AI interaction.

* Round 1: Prompt → AI output → Your evaluation
* Round 2: Revised prompt or approach → AI output → Your evaluation

**Can I use screenshots from my AI tool?** Yes, absolutely. Screenshots of prompts, outputs, and your annotations are great evidence. Just make sure you anonymize any confidential information before including screenshots.

**What if my workflow involved other people (like asking a colleague for feedback)?** That's fine as long as **you** ran the AI workflow and **you** did the human-AI collaboration work. Getting feedback from a colleague on your outputs is acceptable. Having a colleague co-author the submission is not.

## 🔍 Type C: Accessibility Audit + AI Redesign — Specific FAQs

**What should I audit?** You can audit any process, workflow, document, form, webpage, email template, training module, or communication pattern that has accessibility or equity gaps. It can be:

* **Internal to Salesforce** (e.g., a team onboarding process, a performance review template, a Slack communication norm)
* **External** (e.g., a public-facing form, a government benefits document, a website)

If you audit something internal, anonymize it if needed. Do not include confidential Salesforce data in your submission.

**What framework should I use for the audit?** Use a recognized accessibility or equity framework, such as:

* **WCAG 2.1 AA** — Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (for digital content)
* **Plain Language Guidelines** — plainlanguage.gov (for written documents)
* **Section 508** — U.S. federal accessibility standards
* **Equity design frameworks** — e.g., Design Justice principles

**How many accessibility issues do I need to find?** Minimum: **3 substantive findings.** Quality matters more than quantity. Three well-documented, impactful findings are better than ten shallow ones.

**Can I audit something I helped create?** Yes, but be honest about your role. Self-critique can be powerful.

**What if the AI redesign isn't perfect?** That's expected. Show what the AI generated, what you accepted, what you modified, and what you rejected. Judges are evaluating **your judgment**, not the AI's output quality.

**Do I need to implement the redesign?** No. You're showing a before-and-after comparison and explaining the changes. A side-by-side comparison is sufficient.

# 💡 Tips for Success

* **Start with the problem, not the AI tool.** The strongest submissions identify a specific, real problem first, then show how AI helps solve it. Lead with the challenge.
* **Show your work.** Judges want to see your thinking process — prompts, iterations, decisions, course-corrections. A submission that shows honest struggle and adaptation is more compelling than one that claims everything worked perfectly.
* **Be specific about who benefits.** "People who face barriers" is vague. "Employees with visual impairments navigating our onboarding process" is specific. Name the population, the barrier, and the consequence.
* **Don't try to solve everything.** A focused submission that deeply addresses one aspect of a challenge will score higher than a broad submission that gestures at many issues without depth.
* **For the AI Fluency Track, apply the Skill Builder frameworks explicitly.** Don't just mention that you used a Skill Builder - show how it shaped your thinking. Make the connection visible.
* **Pro-tip for the Builder track:** If you build an agent for the hackathon [that is packagable], you can publish your creation on AgentExchange as part of the Salesforce Labs program! Check out the <#C01GGDZ1CUV> channel for all the details on how to get started. :rocket:
